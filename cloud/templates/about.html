“Because of its reliability and ubiquity, the cloud is a particularly mute piece of infrastructure. It is just there, atmospheric and part of the environment.”
Tung-Hui Hu, A Prehistory of the Cloud

A cloud is vaporous, something you can't touch. The cloud is not: it's made out of servers and wires, metal and plastic. Like all corporeal things, this cloud has an impact on the world at large.

The Information and Communication Technology industry accounts for between 1.8 - 3.9% of global carbon emissions. Every action we take in the digital world — from buying a new phone to running a modem — contributes to that figure. Climate change is a systemic issue, so it can be challenging to identify the impacts of an individual life.

This application allows you to see the carbon footprints of various digital activities. Scroll through to see the carbon emissions of everyday activities.

View the code and research on GitHub.
